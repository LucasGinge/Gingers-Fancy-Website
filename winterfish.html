WINTER FISH RECIPES

PAN-ROASTED MACKEREL, WITH BLOOD ORANGE-BRAISED CHICORY

2 fillets mackerel person
1 head chicory per person (white or red)
½ blood orange per person
Glug of good quality white wine
Fresh thyme

*Lightly cure* the mackerel fillets and leave in the fridge for a few hours.

Next, grate the orange peel using a fine grater (preferably a microplane) and put it to one side. Squeeze the juice from the oranges and put in a small saucepan along with a glass of white wine. Bring to a low boil.

Now, slice your chicory lengthways. Get a frying pan on a high heat. Place a small amount of oil in the pan. When smoking, put the chicory flat-side down followed by a few small knobs of butter. When your chicory has nicely browned (about 2 minutes), add a few picked thyme leaves, and the orange juice/wine mixture. Drop to a medium heat and place a lid on the pan.

After ten minutes, your chicory will be cooked. Plate the chicory and put the remaining orange/wine mixture to one side.

Get a frying pan hot. Pat dry the mackerel and lightly coat in oil. Place skin-side down in the pan, remembering to place them away from you so you don’t splash hot oil over your hand. Drop to a medium heat. *Add a small knob of butter*. The fillets will curl. Use a spatula to lightly press them down, flattening the skin against the pan. They will remain flat after a few seconds of holding them there.

After 2 minutes on the skin side, flip the fillets over. Give them a minute on the flesh side then plate them up with the chicory. Once all your mackerel fillets are cooked, pour out any remaining fat from the pan and put it back on a high heat. Add the juice/wine mixture we reserved from the chicory. Keep the heat high so that it quickly reduces. Add the orange peel and a small knob of butter. Mix it thoroughly and pour it over the mackerel. Serve.

COD, WITH CEREIAC REMOULADE

1 cod loin per person
¼ celeriac per person
1 small banana shallot per person
Mayo
Dijon mustard
Chives

*Lightly cure* the cod fillets and leave in the fridge for a few hours

Grate the celeriac and thinly slice the shallot. Mix together with a pinch of salt, a generous spoonful of mustard, a squeeze of lemon, chopped chives, and enough mayo to lightly coat.

Get an oven-proof frying pan nice and hot. Pat the cod fillets dry and lightly coat in oil. Put them in to hot pan, remembering to place them away from you so you don’t splash hot oil over your hand. Put the pan in to a pre-heated oven at 200C for 6 minutes. Serve with the remoulade.
