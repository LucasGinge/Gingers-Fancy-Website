AUTUMN VEG RECIPES

CAPONATA (GINGER'S FAVOURITE)

¼ each aubergine, courgette, and bell pepper per person
¼ red onion per person
Chopped garlic
Basil
*Tomato sauce from tips*

The day before you intend to eat, chop the aubergine, courgette, and pepper in to roughly 2cm pieces. *Generously cure* them and leave them in a container the fridge for at least 24 hours. The salt will draw out a lot of water.

After 24 hours, strain the vegetables. Discard the water. Now wash the excess salt off the vegetables. I usually just put them in the sink, fill it up with cold water, and move them around a bit in the water. Strain them again and drain them well, make sure they’re nice and dry before you cook them.

Get a pan with some olive oil to a medium heat and saute the onions. When soft, add the garlic. Ginger also adds a tonne of chilli. When it’s all soft, add the vegetables and cook at a low heat, stirring regularly. Get some tomato sauce hot in a separate pan and, when the vegetables are cooked, add enough of the tomato sauce to coat all of the vegetables. Add a generous glug of olive oil and mix it all together. Serve with torn basil, and focaccia. You're welcome.

ROASTED SQUASH, WITH GNOCCHI, BROWN BUTTER, AND SAGE

¼ squash per person
Bunch sage
Gnocchi

A couple of hours ahead of cooking time, drop the gnocchi in to a pan of boiling salted water. When they float, scoop in to ice water. Strain them, allow them to dry, and place them in a bowl. Toss them in olive oil, then put them in the fridge.

Peel, de-seed, and chop the squash in to bite-sized pieces. Coat in olive oil, season, and bake at 200C for about 25-30 minutes.

Pick the sage. Place a generous knob of butter in a saucepan with the sage stems. Melt this and leave at a medium heat until the butter is a golden brown colour. Remove the sage stems, which have infused the butter, then put to the side. Chop the sage leaves and put to one side.

Get a frying pan to a medium heat and add a little oil. When the pan is hot, add a handful at a time of gnocchi. The gnocchi will go golden brown and get a beautiful bite on the outside, a perfect combination of chewy and crisp. Once all the gnocchi are browned, get the brown butter in the pan along with the chopped sage. When the sage starts to fry, add it to the gnocchi and toss it in a large bowl. When the gnocchi are all coated in butter, gently mix in your squash and serve.
