CAULIFLOWER CHEESE

¼ cauliflower per person
100g butter
100g plain flour
1l whole milk
250g grated cheddar

Put the milk in a saucepan with half an onion, a bay leaf, a few black peppercorns, and a sprig or two of thyme. Slowly bring to a boil. When it’s just starting to boil, drop the heat to as low as possible.

Drop the chopped cauliflower in to a pan of boiling salted water. After 3 minutes, scoop out and in to ice water. Strain and leave to the side.

Melt the butter in a pan on a low heat, and add the flour. Mix together. This is a roux. Keep mixing it until it goes kind of sandy. At this point, strain your milk, discarding the onion and herbs. Turn the heat up in the roux and pour in a third of the milk, mixing thoroughly with a whisk or spatula. When there are no lumps, add another third of the milk, again whisking until smooth. Add the final third of milk, and whisk it until it has no lumps. You now have béchamel. Take it off the heat.

Place your cauliflower in to an oven-proof dish. After the béchamel has had 10 minutes to cool down, add most of the cheese and mix it thoroughly. You can mix in a generous spoon of mustard at this point, up to you. Pour enough of this mix over the cauliflower to cover. Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top and stick it in a pre-heated 200c oven for 30 minutes. Bliss. This is why santa invented cauliflower.
